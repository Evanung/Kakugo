Why Write?

Writing is one of the key pillars towards fluency.

Writing Exposes Knowledge Gaps

You might recognize a word when you read it, but freeze when it comes time to use it. That is a knowledge gap. Practicing language output is as important as input.

Reinforce what you learn through use

Flashcards build familiarity. Writing builds fluency. What you write forms connections to grammar and vocab for better recall and use.

Writing prepares you for real life situations

As you write in a variety of topics, you learn how to navigate those conversations since you practiced

Prompts

Check out example prompts from our library and see how they can help you!

Why did you start learning Japanese?

Practice story telling structure by giving context, building to a point, and wrapping it up. Express your hopes and dreams in particular subjects.

Write an email apologizing to your boss for a mistake

Learn how to structure an email in Japanese. Gain confidence in writing Japanese keigo. If working in Japan is a plan for you, this is a skill worth practicing

Talk about a culture shock you have experienced before

Learn to express past experiences you have had while also learning about other's experiences as well. Be able to express how you felt and compare cultures

Your house is on fire, what are the three things (besides necessities) that you grab?

Learn to speculate about random topics and defend your choices. Reinforce conditional and hypothetical grammar. Vocabulary about everyday objects that are important to you.
